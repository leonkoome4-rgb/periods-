What is a Menstrual Cycle?

A menstrual cycle is the monthly process that prepares a womanâ€™s body for pregnancy. It starts on the first day of a period and ends on the day before the next period begins. The average menstrual cycle lasts about 28 days, but it can be shorter or longer depending on the person.

How Do You Calculate Your Next Period?

To estimate the next period date, you need to know:

  • The first day of your last period
  • Your average cycle length (number of days)

The next period date is calculated by adding the cycle length to the first day of the last period. For example, if your last period started on January 1st and your cycle length is 28 days, your next period is expected around January 29th.

What Are Fertile and Safe Days?

Ovulation usually happens about 14 days before the next period. This is when a woman is most likely to get pregnant. The fertile window includes a few days before ovulation and one or two days after.

Safe days are the days outside the fertile window when the chance of pregnancy is lower. However, this method is not 100% accurate because cycles can change.

About This Website

This website allows users to enter their menstrual details such as:

  • Last period date
  • Average cycle length
  • Period duration

Using simple calculations, the system estimates the next period date and shows fertile and safe days. The information provided is for educational purposes only and should not replace medical advice.

Important Notice

Every body is different. Stress, illness, diet, and lifestyle can affect menstrual cycles. If you experience irregular periods or health concerns, consult a healthcare professional.
